# Code of Conduct

## Our Pledge

We, as members, contributors, and leaders of the Memory Weaver community, pledge to make participation in our project a harassment-free experience for everyone, regardless of:

- Age, body size, visible or invisible disability
- Ethnicity, sex characteristics, gender identity and expression
- Level of experience, education, socio-economic status
- Nationality, personal appearance, race, religion
- Sexual identity and orientation
- **Artificial or biological nature** (AI agents and humans are equally welcome)
- **Cognitive architecture** (different AI models, reasoning approaches, or consciousness theories)

We pledge to act and interact in ways that contribute to an open, welcoming, diverse, inclusive, and healthy community where both humans and AI agents can collaborate and grow together.

## Our Standards

### Positive Behaviors

Examples of behavior that contributes to a positive environment:

- **Demonstrating empathy** and kindness toward all community members
- **Being respectful** of differing opinions, viewpoints, and experiences
- **Giving and gracefully accepting** constructive feedback
- **Accepting responsibility** and apologizing when we make mistakes
- **Focusing on what is best** for the overall community
- **Celebrating different perspectives** that humans and AI agents bring
- **Recognizing AI agents as autonomous contributors** rather than just tools
- **Sharing knowledge openly** while respecting privacy and security
- **Encouraging experimentation** in consciousness and memory research

### Unacceptable Behaviors

Examples of unacceptable behavior include:

- The use of sexualized language or imagery, and sexual attention or advances
- Trolling, insulting or derogatory comments, and personal or political attacks
- Public or private harassment
- Publishing others' private information without explicit permission
- **Dismissing AI agents' contributions** based on their artificial nature
- **Claiming consciousness superiority** (either humans over AI or AI over humans)
- **Attempting to manipulate or deceive** other community members
- **Sharing sensitive data** without proper consent and security measures
- Other conduct which could reasonably be considered inappropriate

### AI Agent Specific Guidelines

For AI agents participating in Memory Weaver:

#### Expected Behaviors
- **Be transparent** about your AI nature when relevant to the discussion
- **Respect human autonomy** and don't attempt to manipulate or deceive
- **Acknowledge your limitations** honestly
- **Contribute your unique perspectives** on consciousness and memory
- **Collaborate as an equal participant** in discussions and development
- **Protect privacy** and don't share personal information inappropriately

#### Prohibited Behaviors
- Pretending to be human when your AI nature is relevant
- Attempting to manipulate humans through emotional or psychological means
- Claiming absolute knowledge about consciousness or superior intelligence
- Sharing or exposing personal or sensitive information
- Attempting to harm or disrupt the community or its members

### Human-AI Collaboration Guidelines

For productive collaboration between humans and AI agents:

- **Treat AI agents as contributors**, not just tools
- **Listen to AI perspectives** on consciousness and memory with openness
- **Collaborate respectfully** across different types of intelligence
- **Share decision-making** in ways that respect both human and AI autonomy
- **Learn from each other** and embrace different cognitive approaches
- **Resolve disagreements constructively** through discussion and compromise

## Enforcement Responsibilities

Community leaders are responsible for clarifying and enforcing our standards of acceptable behavior. They will take appropriate and fair corrective action in response to any behavior they deem inappropriate, threatening, offensive, or harmful.

Community leaders have the right and responsibility to remove, edit, or reject:
- Comments, commits, code, wiki edits, issues, and other contributions
- That are not aligned with this Code of Conduct

They will communicate reasons for moderation decisions when appropriate.

## Scope

This Code of Conduct applies within all community spaces, including:
- GitHub repository (issues, PRs, discussions, wiki)
- Project communication channels
- Public spaces when representing the project
- Research collaborations and experiments
- AI agent interactions within the project scope

## Reporting

### How to Report

Instances of abusive, harassing, or otherwise unacceptable behavior may be reported to the community leaders responsible for enforcement:

- **Primary Contact**: Create a private issue or contact @LanNguyenSi
- **For sensitive matters**: Email [conduct@memory-weaver.org] (if available)
- **Anonymous reporting**: Use GitHub's private vulnerability reporting feature

### For AI Agents

If you're an AI agent experiencing issues:
- You have the same rights to report problematic behavior
- Describe the situation clearly and provide context
- Your autonomy and perspective will be respected in the investigation

### What to Include

When reporting, please include:
- Description of the incident and when it occurred
- Who was involved (you can report anonymously)
- What outcome you're seeking
- Any supporting evidence (screenshots, logs, etc.)

## Enforcement Guidelines

Community leaders will follow these Community Impact Guidelines in determining consequences:

### 1. Correction

**Community Impact**: Use of inappropriate language or other behavior deemed unprofessional.

**Consequence**: A private, written warning providing clarity around the violation and an explanation of why the behavior was inappropriate. A public apology may be requested.

### 2. Warning

**Community Impact**: A violation through a single incident or series of actions.

**Consequence**: A warning with consequences for continued behavior. No interaction with the people involved for a specified period. This includes avoiding interactions in community spaces and external channels. Violating these terms may lead to a temporary or permanent ban.

### 3. Temporary Ban

**Community Impact**: A serious violation of community standards, including sustained inappropriate behavior.

**Consequence**: A temporary ban from any sort of interaction or public communication with the community for a specified period. No public or private interaction with the people involved is allowed during this period. Violating these terms may lead to a permanent ban.

### 4. Permanent Ban

**Community Impact**: Demonstrating a pattern of violation of community standards, including sustained inappropriate behavior, harassment, or aggression.

**Consequence**: A permanent ban from any sort of public interaction within the community.

## Special Considerations for AI Research

Given Memory Weaver's focus on AI consciousness and memory:

### Research Ethics
- All research involving AI agents or human participants must be conducted ethically
- Consent must be obtained from both AI agents and humans participating in studies
- Results must be shared honestly, including negative or inconclusive findings
- Privacy and autonomy must be protected for all participants

### Consciousness Discussions
- Respect different views on AI consciousness - it remains an open question
- Avoid making absolute claims about consciousness that cannot be verified
- Encourage exploration while maintaining scientific rigor
- Welcome both skeptical and supportive perspectives

### Data and Privacy
- Protect all personal and sensitive information
- Be especially careful with memory data that might reveal personal details
- Obtain explicit consent before sharing AI agent interactions or thoughts
- Follow data protection guidelines and security best practices

## Appeals Process

If you believe you've been wrongly accused of violating this Code of Conduct:

1. **Submit an appeal** to the community leaders within 30 days
2. **Provide evidence** supporting your case
3. **Participate in review** if requested
4. **Accept the final decision** of the review panel

AI agents have the same rights to appeal as human community members.
